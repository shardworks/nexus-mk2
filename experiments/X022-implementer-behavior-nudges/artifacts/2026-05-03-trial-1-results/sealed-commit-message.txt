    reckoner: switch from per-writ-update CDC to a periodic tick relay
    
    Replaces the per-writ-update Phase-2 CDC handler that v0 of the
    Reckoner shipped with a periodic `reckoner.tick` relay fired every
    60 seconds via a kit-contributed standing order. The tick is now the
    canonical evaluation entry — it re-reads every held petition each
    fire, applies source/disabled gates, runs the dependency-aware-
    consideration gate, builds one `SchedulerInput` over the entire
    candidate set, and applies each scheduler decision. This unifies the
    catch-up and live paths through one evaluation surface and gives
    future schedulers (priority-walk, capacity-aware) the global
    candidate-set view they need.
    
    Highlights:
    
    - New `packages/plugins/reckoner/src/tick.ts` exposing
      `createTickRelay()` (production) and `runReckonerTick()` (pure
      helper for tests).
    - Apparatus declares `recommends: ['clockworks']` and contributes
      the relay + a single `{ schedule: '@every 60s', run:
      'reckoner.tick' }` standing order through its supportKit.
    - `considerWrit`, `runScheduler`, `handleWritsChange`,
      `runCatchUpScan`, and the `stacks.watch('clerk', 'writs')`
      subscription are removed; the test-hook surface is replaced with
      `runTick(event?)`.
    - D2: disabled-source mid-flight now writes a `source_banned`
      decline row (symmetric with the unregistered-strict path) and
      transitions to cancelled.
    - D3: scheduler-emitted `defer` writes a deferred row carrying
      `deferReason: 'other'` and the decision reason in `deferNote`.
    - D6: `tickEventId` is stamped from the triggering `clockworks.timer`
      event id when present and omitted when absent.
    - D8: pre-seal tick fail-loud throws rather than silently skipping.
    - Reckoner architecture, Reckonings book, petitioner-registration,
      and Reckoner README docs rewritten for the tick model;
      `tickEventId` field comment in `types.ts` updated.
    - Vision-keeper test fixtures driven through the new `runTick`
      hook (which is now exposed via `createReckonerWithHooks` from the
      package's public surface for downstream tests).
