The new stamp-only `petition(writId, extRequest)` form (this commission) reads the writ via `clerk.show(writId)` to check `ext.reckoner` absence and `clerk.isInitial(writ)`, then calls `clerk.setWritExt` which performs its own transactional read-modify-write. Between the helper's read and `setWritExt`'s read there is a small TOCTOU window: a concurrent stamper could write `ext.reckoner` after our absence check but before our setWritExt fires, and our `setWritExt` would silently overwrite it (it merges sub-slots by pluginId without comparing prior values). This mirrors the orphan-window observation already filed against the existing two-step create+stamp form (obs-4 / obs-5 in the contract document). For v0 the window is acceptable and recoverable: the brief explicitly excludes atomic clerk.post()+setWritExt() bundling. If a real concurrent-stamper bug appears, a `clerk.setWritExtIfAbsent(writId, pluginId, value)` primitive on Clerk — transactional read-modify-write that throws if the sub-slot is already present — would close the window cleanly without exposing Stacks transactions to petitioners.
