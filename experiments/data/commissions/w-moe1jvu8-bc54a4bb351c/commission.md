Today a fresh guild has no standing orders at all — SOF events will land in the events book and get processed with zero matching standing orders (the dispatcher correctly flips them to `processed: true` with no dispatches). That is the right framework default, but it means a brand-new guild has no signal that a relay is broken until an operator adds an SOF-handler standing order.

Worth considering as a follow-on: ship a default `{ on: "standing-order.failed", run: "log-stdlib-relay" }` standing order in the Clockworks apparatus's bundle so first-boot guilds get a baseline log-to-stderr handler. The patron can override later. Not in scope for this commission — it is a defaults/UX question, not a correctness question. Tactical detail: the bundle-merge mechanism exists (`Bundles — may ship default standing orders and custom event declarations` in `clockworks.md`); a default order would land in the Clockworks apparatus's `supportKit` config-shape and merge into `guild.json` on initialization.
