The operator-side `clockworks.standingOrders` array is currently validated only on the first `processEvents` sweep (`runDispatchSweep` calls `validateStandingOrders` at `dispatcher.ts:195`). Until something dispatches, malformed operator entries (missing `run:`, dropped sugar, unknown keys) sit silent. The boot-time defensive `parseSchedule` check at `clockworks.ts:653–660` only catches malformed `schedule:` strings — every other shape error is invisible until first dispatch.

This commission introduces a kit-layer that does fail-loud at boot. Once that lands, the asymmetry — kit failures fail boot, operator failures fail first dispatch — is more visible, and an operator who edits guild.json incorrectly can run for hours before anything tries to dispatch. Worth a follow-up commission: invoke `validateStandingOrders(operatorOrders)` once at apparatus start (after the kit-layer build, before the schedule-seed loop) so the entire standing-order surface fails loud at boot. Affects `packages/plugins/clockworks/src/clockworks.ts` start() body and the integration test fixtures that today exercise the dispatcher's revalidation path.
