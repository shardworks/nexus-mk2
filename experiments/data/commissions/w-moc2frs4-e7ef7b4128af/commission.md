D9 keeps the Actions column on every row (including deep descendants). A grandchild of the detail root being visually adjacent to its grandparent means an operator glancing at the 'Cancel' button may not immediately register which writ they're about to cancel. The main list has the same property after the parent brief, so this is not a net-new risk, but the detail view's narrower context makes the mis-identification slightly more likely. Worth an observability pass during implementation — e.g. confirming that the row-click-vs-button-click hitbox is unambiguous at deep indents, and that the confirm dialog title includes the writ title/id. The existing row-action flow shows only `Resolution (optional)` in the confirm prompt without naming which writ is being cancelled; this was fine at depth 1 and questionable at depth 8.
