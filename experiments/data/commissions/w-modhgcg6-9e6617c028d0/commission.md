Today the Clerk consumes two kit-contributed registries the same way: `consumes: ['writTypes', 'linkKinds']` (clerk.ts L835) with parallel `registerKitWritTypes` and `registerKitLinkKinds` scans in start(). T2 removes the writTypes kit channel entirely (S6/D15) but leaves linkKinds untouched. The asymmetry is intentional — link kinds are a metadata vocabulary, writ types are state machines that need active code-driven registration — but it'll surprise the next plugin author who reads the apparatus declaration and asks why one channel exists and the other doesn't. Capture as either (a) a doc note in clerk.md explaining the asymmetry, or (b) a deeper follow-up commission asking whether linkKinds should also move to a code-driven registration API for symmetry.
