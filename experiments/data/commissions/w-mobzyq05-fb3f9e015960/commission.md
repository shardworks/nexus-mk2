`RigTemplateRegistry.registerKitMappings` in `packages/plugins/spider/src/spider.ts:1100-1122` uses 'first-registered-wins' semantics with a `console.warn` on collision, and kit registration order comes from `wireKitEntries` walking topo-sorted apparatuses. Two kits that collide and neither `requires` the other behave non-deterministically across guild.json plugin orderings. Today this is harmless because no production kit collides, but the brief identifies this as a latent hazard (`the winner must be deterministic, not load-order-dependent`). The commission closes one specific collision (mandate) but does not re-open the general rule; a follow-up that codifies deterministic kit-vs-kit precedence (or replaces the warning with a hard failure) would remove a class of latent bugs. Recommend commissioning design work on a deterministic kit precedence rule rather than special-casing each collision as it arises.
