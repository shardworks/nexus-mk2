`packages/plugins/spider/src/types.ts` declares engine states (`pending | running | completed | failed | cancelled | skipped`) and `packages/plugins/astrolabe/src/types.ts` declares plan statuses (`reading | analyzing | reviewing | writing | completed | failed`). Both are hardcoded TypeScript unions today. The writ-type config schema T1 ships is generic enough to describe these lifecycles without writ-specific entanglement, which suggests a future refactor could lift `WritTypeConfig` + `validateWritTypeConfig` into a shared package (e.g. `@shardworks/state-machine-config`) that Clerk, Spider, and Astrolabe consume. Brief explicitly out-of-scope for this refactor; worth capturing so the generalization isn't silently foreclosed by the T1 naming (`WritTypeConfig` becomes awkward if used for engines). Preserve by keeping the module pure and writ-agnostic in its internal logic.
