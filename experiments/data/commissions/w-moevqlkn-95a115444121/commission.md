`packages/plugins/clockworks/src/clockworks.ts:420-449` registers a Phase-2 CDC watcher on every plugin-declared book (other than `clockworks/events`). Each row mutation re-emits a `book.<ownerId>.<book>.<verb>` event into the events book, where the dispatcher then picks it up. Because the CDC watcher is Phase 2 (`failOnError: false`) and runs *after* the originating transaction commits in the *same process*, this path is exactly-once per originating transaction by Stacks's contract (`docs/architecture/apparatus/stacks.md:270-283`). A reasonable reader of the new at-most-twice contract may worry that book CDC events also double-fire under cross-process dispatch — they don't, because the *emit* is per-transaction-exactly-once; the *dispatch* of the resulting event is at-most-twice. The contract's audience needs the distinction spelled out, or examples of relays that listen to `book.*` events will be miscategorised.
