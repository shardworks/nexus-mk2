`docs/architecture/reckonings-book.md` §'Writer-enforced invariant' states that the iff-outcome invariant ('a row populates exactly the reason fields keyed to its outcome and no others') is enforced only by the Reckoner-core writer, not by the Stacks book schema or any read-time validator. A Workflow-1 caller writing directly to the `reckoner/reckonings` book could in principle persist a malformed row (e.g. an `accepted` row carrying a stray `declineReason`). The framework has no apparatus-isolation enforcement: any plugin with a Stacks handle can write to any book it knows the name of. Consider adding a runtime validator inside the Reckoner that wraps `book.put` for own-row writes; an explicit tripwire for cross-plugin writes (other plugins writing to `reckoner/reckonings`) is harder — a future Stacks-side ownership-check would be the right layer.
