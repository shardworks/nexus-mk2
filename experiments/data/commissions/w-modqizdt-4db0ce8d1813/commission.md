The legacy Clerk cascade had two directions: upward (child failed → parent failed) and downward (parent reached `failed`/`cancelled` → cancel non-terminal children with the canonical resolution string `Automatically cancelled due to parent termination`). T3's brief explicitly excludes downward cascade (`Out of scope: Non-terminal cascade`). After T3 ships, an operator who cancels a parent mandate will leave its non-terminal children running independently — a behaviour change from the pre-T2 baseline that may surprise users.

The `WritTypeChildrenBehavior` shape has no field for a downward action today; restoring this behaviour requires extending the schema (e.g. a `parentTerminal` trigger) and adding the corresponding engine path. Spider's `piece-pipeline.test.ts:709–722` includes a regression that simulates 'the parent's downward cascade beat it to the writ', referencing behaviour that T3 will not provide. Worth a follow-up commission once T3 settles.
