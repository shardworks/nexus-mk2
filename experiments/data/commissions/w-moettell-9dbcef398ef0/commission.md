`packages/plugins/spider/src/spider.ts:3219` runs `clerk.transition(rig.writId, 'completed', { resolution })` when a rig's terminal `seal` engine reports completed. The only guard is `writAlreadyTerminal`. If the writ executing the rig has child writs that are still non-terminal at the moment of seal (e.g. the rig posted sub-mandates that have not yet themselves terminated), the parent→completed transition will land before its children. After the D3 tripwire lands, this throw will roll back the rig’s terminal transition — surfacing a real workflow gap. Recommend a follow-up audit of every Spider rig whose engines `clerk.post()` or otherwise create writ children: those rigs need either (a) a wait-for-children semantic before driving the writ to completed, or (b) explicit acknowledgement that they're not allowed to spawn long-lived children. This is broader than this commission and should be tracked as its own follow-up.
