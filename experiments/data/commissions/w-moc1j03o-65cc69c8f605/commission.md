Per D7, the Reckoner's drain definition is `0 open writs AND 0 active rigs`. A stuck-but-retryable writ will flip back to `open` within one clockworks-retry CDC tick. There is a millisecond-scale window where a terminal transition triggers a drain check, finds nothing else open, and emits a drain pulse — just before the retry clockwork re-opens the writ. The next terminal transition's check will emit another drain when that retry truly drains. For MVP this noise is acceptable (rare, self-correcting). Future enhancement: gate drain emission behind 'also no stuck-retryable-under-cap writs exist', or debounce drain pulses. Record for post-MVP.
