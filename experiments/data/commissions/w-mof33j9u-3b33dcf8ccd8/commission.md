Spider's `start()` in `packages/plugins/spider/src/spider.ts` runs `validateTemplates(spiderConfig.rigTemplates, fabricator)` for templates and validates rig-template mappings, but the simpler scalar fields — `pollIntervalMs`, `maxEnginesPerRig`, `maxConcurrentEngines`, `maxConcurrentEnginesPerRig` — are read with `?? <default>` at the call site (lines 1900, 2098–2099, 2621). A misspelled or wrongly-typed value (e.g. `maxConcurrentEngines: "3"` as a string) would silently fall through to the default.

This is not introduced by the override commission, but adding `engineRetryOverrides` raises the question: should Spider's startup do a uniform fail-loud sweep over all of `SpiderConfig`? The animator has a single fail-loud validator; spider could grow the same.

Follow-up: add a `validateSpiderConfig(raw)` helper that does fail-loud type/range checks on every numeric scalar in `SpiderConfig` (`pollIntervalMs`, `maxEnginesPerRig`, `maxConcurrentEngines`, `maxConcurrentEnginesPerRig`) at startup, mirroring the animator's `validateBackoffConfig`. Today's `?? <default>` call sites can stay; the validator just guarantees those defaults only fire when the field is genuinely absent.
