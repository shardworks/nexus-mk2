The existing CDC handler emits only 'source_unregistered' declines; the Reckonings-book contract document (`docs/architecture/reckonings-book.md:425-450`) describes the narrow enum and explicitly notes 'The decline-reason enum is intentionally narrow ... "this work isn't worth doing on merit" is patron territory.' A scheduler-emitted decline does not fit cleanly into the existing enum members, so 'other' is the catch-all path.

For the future when a real non-trivial scheduler (e.g. priority-walk with policy gates) ships, the right contract decision is whether to add a 'scheduler_declined' enum value to `ReckoningDeclineReason` (in `packages/plugins/reckoner/src/types.ts:225-231`) and update the reckonings-book doc's narrow-enum framing. The substrate commission lands the 'other' shim; the deferred decision tracks for when scheduler-emitted declines become a real workflow.
