Adding `resolution?: string` to `WritTypeChildrenBehaviorAction` (per D3) creates two mutually exclusive ways to set the target's resolution: `copyResolution: true` (dynamic, copied from triggering writ) and `resolution: 'static string'` (literal). The XOR is intuitive but unusual for the existing validator pattern — every other shape rule in `validateWritTypeConfig` is independent. This is the first XOR rule. Worth reviewing the validator's error-message convention to keep the XOR feedback in the same `[clerk] writTypeConfig.<path>: <problem>` shape; e.g. `[clerk] writTypeConfig.childrenBehavior.parentTerminal: copyResolution and resolution are mutually exclusive`. Adjacent triggers (`allSuccess`, `anyFailure`) could reasonably grow `resolution: 'static'` semantics over time — the brief is silent but the pattern generalises.
