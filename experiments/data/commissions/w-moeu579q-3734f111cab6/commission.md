The current `docs/architecture/apparatus/spider.md` does not describe BlockTypes at all, but they are now a load-bearing part of the dispatch predicate (the `holdReason` field on a pending engine is a registered BlockType id; `evaluateDispatchPredicate` consults `BlockType.check()` to decide whether to clear the hold).

The registry already ships five built-ins (`writ-phase`, `scheduled-time`, `book-updated`, `patron-input`, `animator-paused`) under `packages/plugins/spider/src/block-types/`, with the `BlockType` / `CheckResult` / `BlockTypeInfo` types defined in `packages/plugins/spider/src/types.ts` (lines 433–493). The `block-types` tool exists in `packages/plugins/spider/src/tools/block-types.ts` but the doc does not mention it.

This commission's refresh will name `animator-paused` parenthetically as the rate-limit gate, but stops short of describing the registry shape, kit-contribution path, or other built-ins. A follow-up commission should add a 'Block types' top-level section between Engine Failure and CDC Handlers covering: registry and kit-contribution mechanics; the `check() → 'cleared' | 'pending' | 'failed'` contract; the `pollIntervalMs` semantics against `lastCheckedAt`; the five built-ins with one-line summaries; and the `nsg block-types` operator tool.
