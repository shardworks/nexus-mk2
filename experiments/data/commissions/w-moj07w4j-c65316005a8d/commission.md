D21 selected mapping `outcome: 'defer'` from a scheduler decision to 'no transition, no Reckonings row' for v0. The `docs/architecture/reckonings-book.md` contract document (lines 524-541, 282-302) describes a deferred Reckonings row carrying `deferReason`, `deferUntil?`, `deferSignal?`, `deferCount`, `firstDeferredAt`, `lastDeferredAt`, `deferNote?`. None of these fields are computable from a SchedulerDecision alone — they require the scheduler to surface structured deferral metadata that the v0 SchedulerDecision shape does not declare.

The v0 'no row on defer' policy preserves correctness ('absence of row is itself a signal' per the reckonings-book contract) but means a real defer-capable scheduler will produce silent holds with no audit trail. When a deferral-aware scheduler ships, the SchedulerDecision shape needs extension fields for deferReason / deferUntil / deferSignal so the row writer can populate the journal columns. Track for the priority-walk scheduler commission or whichever scheduler first claims defer semantics.
