The Ratchet enforces immutability invariants in its API layer (`goal` can't change, `createdAt` can't change, etc.), but Stacks' `book.patch()` will happily overwrite any field it is handed. A bug in a future Ratchet method that accidentally forwards `createdAt` into a patch would silently corrupt the record. This is the same concern called out in `docs/architecture/apparatus/ratchet.md:358` ("enforced in the Ratchet plugin, not in Stacks"). A lightweight defense would be a `patchWithGuard(fields, allowed)` helper in Ratchet that throws if a caller tries to patch a denylisted field like `id` or `createdAt`. Not required for this commission — but the invariant will get weaker over time as more methods are added.
