Today `packages/plugins/claude-code/src/babysitter.ts:615-646` (`redirectStderrToFile`) redirects the entire `claude` process stderr to a local per-session log file (`<logDir>/<sessionId>.log`). The guild daemon never sees those bytes. The rate-limit-scheduling commission is likely to introduce a small cherry-pick — a stderr pattern-matcher or NDJSON inspection — but the broader question of 'the guild should be able to read provider stderr for diagnosis' is an independently valuable refactor. A follow-up should add an opt-in stderr forwarding channel (e.g. a tail-N-bytes field on `session-record`, or a dedicated SQLite table) so Laboratory / patrons can retrieve the signal without SSH-ing to the host. Out of scope here because it affects sessions beyond rate-limit ones and would double the session-record payload shape.
