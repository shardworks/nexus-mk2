Stacks caps Phase 1 cascade depth at 16 (`packages/plugins/stacks/src/stacks-core.ts:39`). T3 inherits this cap implicitly: a 17-deep mandate hierarchy whose leaf transitions to terminal would throw `[stacks] Maximum cascade depth (16) exceeded` and roll the entire commit back. The error message attributes the failure to 'a CDC handler is triggering itself recursively', which would mislead an operator triaging a deep but legal hierarchy.

T3 itself doesn't change this cap, but the cap interacts with cascade in a way the existing wording doesn't anticipate (mandate → piece → sub-piece … 17 deep is conceivable in long-running plans). Worth: (a) raising the cap, (b) refining the error message to distinguish 'depth exhausted by legal hierarchy' from 'reentrant loop', or (c) documenting the cap explicitly in the children-behavior engine docs so operators have a heads-up. Out of scope for T3.
