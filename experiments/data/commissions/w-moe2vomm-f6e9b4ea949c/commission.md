Decision D5 keeps Animator/Loom under `recommends`, with lazy resolution at handler-call time. That is the right footprint trade-off, but it pushes the failure all the way to first dispatch — an operator sees `clockworks: animator apparatus is not installed` only when an event actually fires their summon-relay standing order, possibly hours into the daemon's lifetime.

A cleaner experience: at apparatus `start()`, walk the configured `clockworks.standingOrders` array; for any order with `run: 'summon-relay'` (or whatever D1 settles on), log a startup warning if `animator` or `loom` is absent. This is policy at the boundary, not a runtime invariant change — the relay still throws cleanly at call time — but it surfaces the dependency mismatch when the operator is most likely to fix it (boot time).
