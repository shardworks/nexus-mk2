## By Experiment

### X006 — Guild Metaphor

**"Huge mechanical installations" as the naming register principle.** For the first time,
Sean articulated a positive principle for apparatus naming — not just "this feels wrong"
but "this is what right looks like." The register is: giant, convoluted, mechanised things.
Walker was off because it sounds like a role or a motion. Spider works because it implies
a complex mechanical presence with reach and articulation.

**The apparatus naming fracture is significant and acknowledged.** Parlour, Stacks,
Scriptorium = places. Clerk, Curator = people. Neither category fits the principle. He
counts them — "the majority of apparatus names." Spider moves one in the right direction.
The principle exists; the vocabulary doesn't match it yet.

**The irritation is real.** "Definitely irritating" — not tolerated with equanimity but
with deliberate patience. The experimental discipline (don't rename until metaphor
experiments run) is creating a mild ongoing friction. Worth noting whether this changes
the baseline energy once cleanup is permitted.

### X008 — Patron's Hands

**The review layers as process visibility proxy.** The quality scorer and Coco together
surfaced the Walker Increment 3 architectural debt that Sean says he "probably wouldn't
have reviewed manually" with the same depth. This is the middle path pattern from the
last interview crystallizing further: not real-time visibility, not pure output judgment —
a structured post-hoc review layer that enables evaluation depth beyond what Sean would
sustain alone.

**"Not sure if I would be reviewing anima code and how much if it were all manual."** This
is a significant admission. The scorer isn't just confirming Sean's reads — it's enabling
a review discipline he might not maintain without it. H3 (seeing vs judging) may have a
third path: structured post-hoc review channels that give calibration without real-time
visibility.

**Documentation ownership as methodological control, not just trust.** "If they also
create the spec, that becomes a meaningless test." Sean and Coco owning the docs is a
principled experimental design decision: preserve the docs as an independent evaluation
baseline against which anima work can be measured. This will change when the system has
adversarial quality roles, but for now it's the control condition.

## Cross-Cutting Observations

**First-load failure as evaluation primer.** The dashboard's broken first launch is a
practical finding about commission evaluation: a broken initial experience primes negative
reading of everything that follows. "Probably a lesson for demos that work in there
somewhere" — Sean connecting it to presentation contexts, but it applies equally to
commission review. The order of discovery shapes the judgment.

**Review infrastructure enabling evaluation discipline.** The scorer + Coco review layer
is not just a quality signal — it's enabling a review depth Sean wouldn't sustain manually.
This has a design implication: evaluation quality correlates with infrastructure quality,
not just patron discipline. This is a running theme emerging across X008 and X013.

**Documentation as experimental control.** "Coco and I own the docs" because docs are the
benchmark. The reasoning is sophisticated: it's not "I don't trust them" but "if they write
the criteria and meet the criteria, the test is circular." Sean is thinking about the
research validity of his own system. This may also foreshadow the structural design for
when docs can be commissioned: once adversarial quality roles exist that can evaluate doc
accuracy independently.

**Vocabulary irritation is accumulating.** "Definitely irritating" on apparatus names.
This is not new — it was noted in the last interview. But it's not fading with time; it's
steady background friction. When cleanup becomes available, it may happen quickly.
