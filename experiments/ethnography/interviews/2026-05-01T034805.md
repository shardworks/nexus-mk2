**"Convenient" not "loss."** Retiring the instrument felt like relief from friction, not

betting on the Laboratory apparatus and a larger commission corpus to produce better data
eventually. This is a real bet — qualitative human-experience data (the kind X008 exists

anxiety signal has been supplemented (or partially replaced) by a resource allocation

**H1 (tight loop trap) update.** Running 2-4 commissions in-flight feels "closer" to the
imagined autonomous operation, but "slower than imagined." The tight-loop comparison is no
longer the primary reference point; the reference is now the vision of nimble autonomous

## Cross-Cutting Observations

**The system's costs now shape the system's development.** This is a new feedback loop
that hasn't appeared in prior interviews. Cost pressure is determining what gets
commissioned, which means the research and development agenda is being set by the
economics of the current architecture. That's a significant constraint on what "autonomous
operation" can mean in practice today.

**Research instrumentation slipping, bet placed on automation.** The complexity rating
joins the commission-log review ergonomics as an instrument that was abandoned under
workflow friction. Sean is aware of this and framing it as a bet — the hypothesis is that
automated data capture will produce more and better data than manual instrumentation ever
did. This may be right about quantity; it's probably wrong about the qualitative human
experience data that ethnography is designed to capture.

**"Nimble" is the target state word.** Sean used it to describe what the system isn't yet.
The vision isn't just "autonomous" — it's fast autonomous. The wall-time constraint is
part of what makes the current state feel like a waypoint, not an endpoint.
