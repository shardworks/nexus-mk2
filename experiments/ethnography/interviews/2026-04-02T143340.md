## By Experiment

### X006 — Guild Metaphor

No direct metaphor questions this session. One data point: no mention of vocabulary
friction despite a full day of commission work. The friction from the last interview
(register tax, apparatus naming cracks) didn't surface today. Either it's less salient
when working in the operational layer, or it's genuinely background noise now.

### X008 — Patron's Hands

**Coco's role is self-reorganizing.** The anticipated "clean shift" — from interactive
implementation to pipeline dispatch with Coco as reviewer — is emerging as Sean's own
expectation, not externally prescribed. He's articulating the transition without
prompting. This is consistent with the H1 signal from the last interview: the tight loop
may be self-loosening.

**The "draft implementations" framing.** "Pretty good at creating draft implementations
and iterating" positions autonomous agents as draft producers, not final-output producers.
Coco's role in the new model is to close the gap between draft and shippable. This is a
stable operating pattern, not a failing one — but it's also not the eventual target state
(self-directed improvement loops, system evaluating its own outputs without Coco routing).

**Partial outcomes as tech debt accelerators.** The concern about accumulated cruft from
repeated iteration is a new signal for X008 H3 (seeing vs. judging). When you only see
outputs, you can't observe whether the iteration path produced clean or messy intermediate
states. The suspicion that "more iterations = more debt" may become a structuring concern
for how Sean evaluates commission quality — not just "did it succeed" but "how many times
did it have to try."

### X013 — Commission Outcomes

**Normalize-IDs partial: framing trap identified.** The spec had Clerk in the table; the
framing "make everything work like Clerk" gave the anima logical cover to skip Clerk
changes. The information was present; the implication wasn't explicit enough. This is a
specific failure mode that's hard to prescribe against: not missing information, but
wording that licenses an incomplete reading.

**The ceiling on patron craft.** Sean's explicit statement that he can't reliably catch
subtle implication traps at spec-writing time puts a ceiling on how much "better specs"
can improve outcomes. This is significant for H1: spec quality is a predictor, but there's
a floor of spec quality that humans can't reliably exceed. Planning and review cycles are
the structural answer — build a step that catches what the patron misses.

**Abandoned outcome reframed as preferable.** The normalize-IDs first dispatch (abandoned,
no commit) is now explicitly better than a bad partial landing in the codebase. This is a
useful calibration: not all non-successes are equal; abandoned-clean may be preferable to
partial-messy. Worth tracking whether this holds as the commission volume grows.

**Quality scorer: trusted, calibrated, slightly raises the floor.** Three properties: (1)
internally consistent across runs, (2) tracks patron assessment closely, (3) occasionally
flags things the patron would have let slide. This is the right profile for a useful
instrument — not rubber-stamping patron reads, but not diverging so far as to erode trust.

**50% revision rate baseline confirmed emotionally.** The perception that it was "most"
when it was 50% is worth noting. Partial outcomes carry more weight than the raw number
suggests. Revision rate as a health indicator (H3) may understate the patron's subjective
experience of system quality.

## Cross-Cutting Observations

**The structural fix is a pipeline, not a practice.** Every thread in this interview leads
to the same answer: planning and review cycles as built-in pipeline steps, not patron
discipline or spec templates. The quality analysis prescription ("be exhaustive") is
already being resisted; the sustainable version has to be architectural.

**Present-tense capability assessment, not aspiration.** "Pretty good at creating draft
implementations and iterating" is the first time Sean has described the system's actual
capability in settled, present-tense terms. Previous sessions have been forward-looking
("impatient," "hoping"). Something has shifted in confidence — probably the quality
analysis making the system's behavior legible.

**The moral concern as background noise.** The AI ethics tension is present and
periodically surfaces, but it doesn't shape day-to-day decisions. Worth noting as a
standing condition rather than a variable.
