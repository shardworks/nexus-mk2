# Interview — 2026-03-25 (pre-dispatch)

## Context

Sixth interview, same day as the fifth. Sean is about to run the first autonomous commission for X007 — the guild monitor web dashboard. He has already written a patron expectations doc and the commission spec. This interview was conducted immediately before he set up the guild to run the commission. The infrastructure is ready; the dispatch is minutes away.

## Key Quotes

**"It actually wasn't too long ago when I wrote that. It's definitely going to be manual still. After this interview will be when I setup the guild to use."**
— On whether the process had become more turnkey since writing the expectations doc. It hadn't. He's going in knowing it will be manual.

**"It's based on my previous experience mostly, things I've been disappointed by agents doing. As I was writing it, I realized that the list of 'things that will go poorly' are probably the start of a list of 'things not to do' I will eventually have to capture in a guild codex."**
— On the source of his failure predictions. The expectations doc was not just prediction — it surfaced as the seed of future codex content. Failure taxonomy as design input.

**"It will be after. I wrote my expectations based on the commission spec and few details we had already pre-planned for the codex (around non-functional requirements for library selection). No harm in getting confirmation on my predictions before pre-fixing the problems."**
— On why he's not writing codex entries now. Deliberate choice to let the commission fail naturally first. "Confirmation before pre-fixing" — he wants evidence, not just hypothesis.

**"Probably just wait. Go for a walk maybe. I can't think of any real-time output we are capturing that can be inspected from the outside of how the agent session is going. So the only thing I could really do is constantly diff the workshop's codebase or something... which I'm not interested in."**
— On what he'll do while the commission runs. No monitoring plan. Not interested in watching the diff. Genuine detachment.

**"Probably a relief. I've been pushing pretty hard on this lately."**
— On whether waiting feels strange or relieving. Brief, flat, unambiguous.

**"I'd be most delighted if it somehow nailed a visual style that I found appealing. I'd be surprised if it published an actual package, but that wouldn't delight me as much as an attractive web page."**
— On what would genuinely surprise him positively. Aesthetics ranked above technical achievement. CI/CD success would be more impressive but less satisfying.

## By Experiment

### X007 — First Contact

This is the pre-dispatch capture. Key observations:

- **Process:** Still manual. Guild setup happens after this interview. Sean is not treating this as a problem — it's just the current state.
- **Failure predictions as codex seed:** The expectations doc's "go poorly" list has already been reframed in Sean's mind as a future codex ("things not to do"). This is the first sign of how X007 output will feed back into the system design.
- **Experimental discipline:** He explicitly chose to not pre-fix the problems he predicts. "No harm in getting confirmation before pre-fixing." He wants to see whether his priors are correct before acting on them.
- **Monitoring:** No plan to watch the commission run. "Go for a walk." The absence of real-time observability is noted but accepted — he's not interested in the alternative (diffing the codebase continuously).
- **The delight question:** When asked what would genuinely surprise him positively, he went immediately to aesthetics — an attractive web page — over technical achievement like package publishing. This is interesting: the technically harder thing (CI/CD) would impress him less than the subjectively harder thing (visual style). His ceiling for AI technical competence is higher than his ceiling for AI aesthetic judgment.

### X008 — Patron's Hands

- The "go for a walk" response is the sharpest contrast yet to the tight-loop working pattern that has defined the past week. Every session note from today shows Sean actively directing Coco through complex decisions. The commission is the first thing he's genuinely stepping away from.
- The relief framing ("I've been pushing pretty hard on this lately") is the first direct acknowledgment of fatigue in the directing role. Worth tracking whether this shapes how he evaluates the commission output — if he's tired, does he apply less scrutiny?

## Cross-Cutting Observations

The failure-prediction-to-codex pipeline is worth tracking. Sean arrived at X007 with a list of things he expects to go wrong, derived from prior experience with AI coding agents. He's now consciously holding that list as "proto-codex content" pending confirmation. This creates a feedback loop from the experiment into the system design: X007 results may directly shape what goes into the guild codex, which shapes what future autonomous agents do. The experiment is not just observational — it's generative of system policy.

The aesthetics-over-technical-achievement preference is a data point about what Sean values in agent output. He expects technical competence (web UI, reasonable libraries) but doesn't find it delightful. He'd be surprised by aesthetic competence and find it more satisfying. This may inform commission writing — if he wants to increase the odds of delight, he might need to be more explicit about visual requirements than he has been.
