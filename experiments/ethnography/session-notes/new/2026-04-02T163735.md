# Session Summary

## What we did

- **Designed The Laboratory** — a purely observational apparatus that uses Stacks CDC to automate experiment data collection. Started from a "what to build next" discussion (Clockworks vs Rigging) and landed on a lighter-weight option that uses existing CDC infrastructure.
- **Scaffolded a TypeScript monorepo** in the sanctum (`nexus-mk2/`) — `package.json`, `tsconfig.json`, `pnpm-workspace.yaml`, mirroring the nexus repo setup.
- **Built the full Laboratory apparatus** (`packages/laboratory/`) — 8 source files, 14 passing tests. Watches the Clerk's writs book and the Animator's sessions book via Phase 2 CDC handlers. On writ creation: creates commission data dir, writes `commission.md` (writ body), writes `review.md` template, appends skeleton to commission log, auto-commits. - **Wrote README** for the Laboratory with full operational details.

## Decisions made and/or deferred

- **Laboratory over Clockworks/Rigging** — CDC gives us observation without the full event dispatch machinery. Nothing is throwaway.
- **Shell out to quality-review-full.sh** rather than reimplementing in TypeScript — use what works.
- **Hand-append YAML** for commission log — preserves comments and formatting.
- **Auto-commit per CDC event**, best-effort (swallow failures).
- **Cut timeline.yaml** — redundant with data already in the Stacks and commission log.
- **Deferred:** idempotency guards, stacks publication as standard practice, guild installation of the Laboratory.
