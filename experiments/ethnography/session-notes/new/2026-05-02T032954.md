### Notable moments

- **Sean caught the cost.** When I reported the distiller burned
  ~9.5M tokens (raw, before deduping) on a 16-engine session
  summary, he said: *"that.. feels like a LOT of tokens for the
  distiller. why was there such a huge cache read?"* The
  investigation surfaced both the duplicated-record artifact in
  the JSONL (2x inflation) AND the real underlying issue (chunked-
  Read multi-turn growth). *Notable*: instinctive cost-awareness
  caught a problem that I had reported and moved on from. The
  pipeline cost should be a routinely-watched number, not a
  one-time discovery.
- **Sean's optimization framing.** *"yea, we should definitely
  prefilter to just chat messages and none of the tool results.
  then include the whole transcript in the prompt."* — both
  optimizations in one sentence. The pre-filter insight is the
  bigger win (drops input ~5-10x before the distiller ever
  sees it); one-shot is a smaller incremental gain on top.
  *Notable for project narrative*: agent-to-agent context handoff
  is a place where careful filtering can dramatically reduce
  cost without losing fidelity, because most of the volume is
  noise the receiving agent doesn't need.
