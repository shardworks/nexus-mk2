## What we did

- Held off on committing to the prior-session proposal (click+body+materialize hybrid) and surveyed prior art across knowledge-management traditions: Zettelkasten, PARA/CODE, BuJo, Cornell, lab notebooks, SOAP, ADR, qualitative-coding memos, distributed cognition, Mueller-Oppenheimer, Anki. Reframed the problem as **substrate vs. artifact** (lab notebook ≠ paper).
- Mapped functions to mechanisms; identified AI-native moves (subagent for fresh-context distillation, automated grounding-checks, programmable hooks) that human knowledge-management disciplines can't access.
- Ran a real A/B test: produced two subagent-generated distills from the same transcript (with vs. without inline BuJo symbols). The annotated version produced *fewer* decisions — symbols may anchor the distiller to chat-time decomposition. Concluded symbols don't help the distiller.
- Built Tier 1 pipeline (commit `2230e366`): distiller + verifier agents (Sonnet), JSONL preprocessor (16x reduction), wrapper writing to `docs/planning/<date>-<slug>.md`. Three concurrent sessions used it during the build.
- Archived the design path (commit `93e75a27`) under `docs/archive/design-sessions/distillation-pipeline-design/`.
- Reverted the pipeline (commit `bdb934e2`) after Sean surfaced two issues: cross-session canonical state was lost (clicks did that job), and the audience for distill files became unclear. Captured the full arc in `00-arc-postmortem.md`.
