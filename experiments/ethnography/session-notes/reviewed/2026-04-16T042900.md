## Decisions made and/or deferred

- **Intent brief format: applied directly** — not commissioned. Sean directed updating sage instructions as operational work, not feature development.
- **Task `type` attribute: dropped** — no taxonomy, no behavioral difference. Tasks are just tasks.
- **Terminal verify task: dropped** — final verification is an engine loop property, not a manifest task. Task list must be freely appendable.
- **`files` semantics: standing rule** — predicted blast radius, not exhaustive. General property of the manifest, not per-task caveat.
- **v2 writ type: `piece`** — "piece of a greater work", contributed by Astrolabe kit.
- **v2 review: after full loop** — reviews mandate as a whole, not per-piece.
- **v2 retry: halt and surface** — simplest safe default. No auto-retry.
- **v2 ordering: linear only** — creation order = manifest order. No DAG.
- **v2 seal: once after review** — not per-piece.
- **Handoff notes: separated from v2** — independent quest (w-mo0znvsz). Structured YAML note from previous task injected into next session prompt.
- **Deferred: preference model (v3)** — needs more design work and correction data from v1/v2 usage.

This reframes implementation from a monolithic session into an iterative, self-correcting process.

### Experiment observations

- The w-mny1zoc9 case study is a strong data point for X008 (spec quality → outcome correlation): the 764-line prescriptive spec scored "strong" on spec_quality_pre but produced a partial outcome due to the rename table omission. Under the intent model, the brief would have been ~80 lines and would have caught the spider consumer via "grep the monorepo" rather than a static file list. Spec quality metrics may need recalibration for intent-based briefs — "strong" under the old model (exhaustive detail) ≠ "strong" under the new model (clear intent + blast radius coverage).
