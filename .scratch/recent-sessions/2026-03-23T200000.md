### Notable moments

- **"it wasn't the _pretentiousness_ of golem, it was the _personess_"** — Sean pinpointing why "golem" and "machine" both felt wrong. Led to the visualization exercise: walking through the guild, these bash scripts aren't servants, they're the building working. Pulleys, waterworks, clockwork in the walls. This produced "engine" — the one word that captured infrastructure-not-entity. Notable because it shows how a naming decision reveals a design insight: the animated/inanimate distinction isn't creature-vs-creature, it's person-vs-infrastructure.
- **"does the lexicon need to exist?"** — Sean challenging structural bloat. The lexicon was pure duplication of the metaphor doc. Removing it and replacing with a reference sharpened the architecture doc's purpose: it describes *how*, the metaphor describes *what*. Notable as ongoing editorial discipline.
- **"can you apply this same test to the other new terms?"** — after Coco's "what functional purpose does teachings serve?" analysis, Sean asked for the same rigor on every new concept. This produced the functional-purpose audit that cut School and Teachings and confirmed everything else. Notable because the patron is teaching the agent how to evaluate, not just what to evaluate.
