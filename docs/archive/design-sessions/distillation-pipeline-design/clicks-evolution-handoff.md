# Clicks evolution — handoff

A condensed summary of the click-system evaluation done in session `clicks-rework`
(2026-04-29). Pick up here.

## Where we landed

We want to evolve the click system, not replace it, by **adding append-only bodies
and materializing the click corpus to disk as markdown**. The shape:

- **Source of truth: structured clicks** (DB-backed), unchanged. Writes stay
  CLI-mediated and append-only — this is the load-bearing ergonomic that drove
  the original click design. We do **not** want to bring back authoring markdown
  files by hand; that's the editing pain that pushed us away from the prior
  thread-doc model.
- **Long-form lives in append-only body entries on clicks.** A click can have
  any number of timestamped + session-stamped body entries added via
  `nsg click body-append --id <id> --content "..."`. Bodies are extended, never
  edited; the immutable-record discipline carries over.
- **Rendered view: `threads/` + `records/` + `todo.md` materialized to disk.**
  Auto-regenerated on every click op (post-write hook or daemon tick). Sean
  browses these in his editor; agents `cat` them. Files are **derived**, not
  authored — never edited by humans.
- **Tight conclusions, enforced.** With body slot available, conclusions
  collapse to the actual decision (1–2 sentences). Likely needs a CLI guardrail
  (warn or cap above ~400–600 chars without a body entry) so discipline doesn't
  regress.

## Why — the problems this addresses

From quantitative analysis of 10 recent transcripts (Apr 24 → May 1):

- Click commands eat ~14% of total tool output per session (range 3.6%–38.4%).
- Startup tree dump averages ~11K chars; only ~1.4% of listed clicks are
  accessed in the same session.
- Conclusions average **1,064 chars** — 7/13 sampled were over 1,000 chars,
  despite the skill saying "tight conclusion, exploration in transcript."
- 286 live clicks, 78% leaves, no aging mechanism.
- Click IDs leak into agent prose 38–54 times per session despite the rule
  "keep IDs out of prose" — there's no good substitute referent.

From Sean's qualitative pain (the more important signal):

1. **No place for larger content.** Clicks were supposed to be short with
   volume substituting for long-form. That assumption was wrong. Long-form
   ends up in `.scratch/` and gets lost, or bloats click goals/conclusions.
2. **Lost / can't verify.** Sean has no mental model of the click tree, can't
   confirm Coco's capture matches his understanding, hence the constant
   "summarize the X clicks" pattern. Trust-but-cannot-verify.
3. **No standardized patterns.** When to click, granularity, child-vs-sibling,
   todo-vs-question — all improvised. Playbook needed.
4. **LLM impedance mismatch.** Models work better with longer-form structured
   markdown than 50 disconnected micro-clicks. `extract` is a partial workaround.
5. **Cost of micro-edit cadence** — ~10–25% of context budget on click
   bookkeeping rather than substantive work.

Plus historical constraint Sean reminded me of: **we previously used a
threads-like markdown format; editing it was cumbersome (Edit-tool finickiness,
anchor hunting in long files). Clicks were a deliberate response to that pain.**
Any proposal must preserve append-only write ergonomics.

## Decisions made this session

### Body vs child click — when to use which

A child click is for a question that needs its own resolution and identity.
A body entry is for content that supports a resolution somewhere else.

**Decision test, in order:**
1. Will this resolve into its own decision? → child click.
2. Will it need to be parked / dropped / superseded / commissioned independently? → child click.
3. Will future work need to reference *this thing by id* from elsewhere in the tree? → child click.
4. Could it be picked up by a different session/agent on its own? → child click.
5. None of the above — it's analysis, evidence, journal, options-comparison? → body entry.

**Sanity check before opening a click:** "If I just appended this to the parent's body instead, what would be lost?" If the answer is *nothing important, just tree-visibility* — body entry, save the click.

**Anti-patterns:**
- Click-as-note-taking ("noticed X", "tried Y") — body entries.
- Body-as-tree-replacement (5K-line body covering many sub-decisions) — decompose.
- Click-explosion under a parent where children all conclude similarly — bottom layer was probably bodies.

### Reframe: threads as rendered view, not authored substrate

My initial proposal was "three artifact types: threads / records / tasks" with threads as primary-authored markdown. Sean correctly pushed back that this reintroduces the editing pain that drove us to clicks. The corrected shape: **threads are derived from clicks, not written.** The export demonstrates that the rendered output is what makes the model work for verifiability + LLM impedance, while the click data layer keeps writes append-only.

### Benefit/drawback assessment

Walked through every benefit (B1–B7), drawback (D1–D7), and Sean's pain points
(P1–P5) under the proposed model. Summary:

- **Strong wins:** P1 (long-form has a home), P2 (verifiability), D2 (conclusion verbosity).
- **Solid wins:** D7 (findability), D3 (ID leakage), B3 (handoff fidelity), D6 (supersedes findability).
- **Untouched:** D4 (live-pile aging), D5 (scriptorium error pollution), P3 (playbook), N6 (author cognitive load — read-side fixed, write-side not).
- **New risks worth taking seriously:** N1 (body discipline regression — without enforcement, conclusion bloat returns); N2 (materialization staleness if daemon falls behind).

All B1–B7 benefits **preserved**, four enhanced. Critically, B4 (append-only
write ergonomics) is preserved — which was the trap I almost walked into.

## Open questions / next decisions

1. **Adopt or reject the proposal.** The whole thing is a recommendation; Sean
   decides. Browse the export first.
2. **Body length convention / cap.** Soft warning? Hard cap? What threshold?
3. **Conclusion length enforcement** (separate from body). Hard cap likely
   needed to prevent N1.
4. **Materialization mechanism.** Daemon-driven? Post-write hook on the CLI?
   On-demand `nsg click materialize`? Eagerness vs. cost trade-off.
5. **Index file shape.** `threads/index.md` listing active topics + status
   counts? A `clicks/active.md` flat-list?
6. **Migration of existing clicks.** Their conclusions are essays. Do we
   retroactively extract bodies? Or accept legacy verbose conclusions and
   apply discipline only to new clicks? Probably the latter.
7. **The playbook itself.** This handoff started one (body-vs-click section).
   The full playbook still needs writing — granularity rules, click types,
   anti-patterns with worked examples, when NOT to click. Inductive (mine the
   existing corpus) or top-down (write theory first)?
8. **Adjacent fixes that aren't this proposal:** scriptorium error squash,
   live-pile aging, default tree to umbrella-scan, batched writes for cost
   reduction. Independent, smaller, all worth doing.
